National Police Memorial

(Professional Project- Junior Architect)

The National Police Memorial commemorates the 34,844 police personnel from all the central and state police forces in India who have laid their lives in the line of duty since the nation's independence in 1947. Built on a wedge-shaped plot measuring 6.12 acres, the National Police Memorial and museum is located on Shanti path at Chanakyapuri, New Delhi. The memorial consists of a 30-foot-tall sculpture on a wreath platform, a large circular ceremonial plaza for daily flag hoisting and other police day functions, the ‘Wall of Valor’ which carries names of martyr’s engraved on panels of black granite slabs, and lastly the National Police Memorial Museum which occupies a specially carved-out space in the basement. The museum traces the origin of policing from prehistoric times to the formation of security forces by the Mughals, to how the institution transformed during the British-era and then independent India.
