Revitalisation of Bindusagar Lake

(Academic Project- Undergraduate thesis)

This is my undergraduate thesis project based on the urban renewal of the temple town of India Located in Bhubaneshwar. The project was a proposal by the government of Odisha in 2014. It primarily aimed at the restoration of a heritage lake and its surrounding temples from its current state of degradation. The project also proposed the redevelopment of the peripheral settlement which is inhabited by several generations of temple priests. It also involved the redefining of public spaces by introducing urban design interventions and city planning policies, that would open up easier access to the lake which would revive the living culture of the old temple town. Additionally, to forge the importance of the heritage zone, a tourist interpretation centre and a Sanskrit school/hindu monastery were proposed to be built on the banks of the Bindusagar lake.

TThe Bindusagar Lake is one of the popular tourist attractions of Bhubaneswar. Every year, the idol of Lord Lingaraja, enshrined in the Lingaraja Temple, is brought to the lake for a ritual bath. The lake serves as a focal point around which numerous temples of Bhubaneswar are located. At one time, over more than thousand temples bordered the Bindusagar. Now only 500 of these still survive and are all built in the traditional Kalinga architectural style. Over time other nonconforming uses such as shops, residential buildings grew up in the available space resulting in a gradual decay of the shrine and the lake which became heavily polluted and lost its self-purifying capacity. Even now, in its dilapidated state, the lake is used by thousands of pilgrims and local people for a holy dip in faith. Over the years, the economic and social linkages have undergone change. Growing population and immigration of other social groups and impact of tourism has an impact on the character of the old town. The old town has gradually transformed into a dormitory town for the administrative capital. Perceptibly the difference is manifest in its unorganized and haphazard character, encroachments, unregulated quarrying, polluted water bodies, congested roads and densely built derelict houses.

Fig2. Bindusagar lake, ghats and Jagati temple at the centre.

The proposed Ekamra Kshetra interpretation centre at Bindusagar lake aims at establishing an institution for the dissemination of information related to the natural and cultural heritage of the old temple town of Bhubaneshwar. The 1500 sqm. building complex consists of exhibition galleries and an audio-visual room. The centre also houses the Kedarnath library, which is an existing library containing ancient manuscripts relocated from its present location across the street. Additionally, landscaped outdoor spaces and food courts facing the lake add to increased interaction with the lake.

The Sanskrit school/Matha (Monastery) is proposed to be built on the matha ghat on the existing site of the Krushna Chandra Gurukul Sanskrit college. The present building lacks the infrastructure to support year-round classes and is currently being used as an examination center. The building is surrounded by numerous small temples, most of which are in a dilapidated condition. Adjacent to the Sanskrit college lies a patch of land with a few temporary hutments. This is the existing Hindu monastery which houses scholars, temple priests and pilgrims.
