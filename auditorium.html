'Naav'

(Auditorium and cultural center) (Academic Project)

This is a studio design-project from my undergraduate years in architecture school. The socio-cultural/institutional campus was designed to serve as a place for interaction and participation of the local community. Apart from being a recreational spot, it also plays a significant role in preserving and promoting local culture. The multipurpose building complex comprises of an auditorium, an art gallery, restaurants, conference rooms, exhibition halls, courtyards, and a dance and music academy. The Auditorium and cultural center are built on 1.9 acres, spread on a 4.8-acre site located at Kalkaji, adjacent to the famous Lotus Temple in New Delhi.

The design philosophy draws inspiration from the Lotus temple and is developed on the idea of ‘a pool of water’. Here ‘Naav’, meaning a boat, suggests that the building is a metaphor for a boat and the natural environment as the river. Our journey through a river depends on the interaction of the river with the boat, similarly, given that we spend major parts of our lives inside buildings, how one feels and interacts with the surroundings determines the quality of their lives.
