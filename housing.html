Housing

(Academic Project)

The Netaji Nagar housing project was a government proposed project aimed at meeting the rising demands of high-rise group housing for its staff. As an academic project we designed an alternate solution that would be more functional, green, and inclusive of the diverse groups of occupants categorized primarily on their incomes. The site was in Chanakya puri, New Delhi and measured approximately 56 Hectares. The different kinds of income groups that were to be housed were HIG (High Income Group), MIG (Middle Income Group), LIG (Low Income Group) and EWS (Economically Weaker Sections). The housing was developed with the idea of ‘self-sufficiency’. Housing needs for the elderly and other public facilities such sports complex, gym, parks, retail, and commercial facilities were also provided. Even horticulture and vegetable gardens were created, enabling the community to grow their own organic food. The central green space is the center of the whole site is the unifying factor of the site despite the stratification in the society. The design of the units was optimum and efficiently planned, keeping in mind the user comfortability of even the smaller units such as the LIG and EWS.
